Few facts about the world we live in

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known astronomical object to harbor life. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, mostly oceans, and the remaining 29% is land, consisting of continents and islands. Earth has a relatively dense atmosphere composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen, which supports a wide variety of life forms and protects the planet from harmful solar radiation.


🔭 Basic Characteristics:

  • Diameter: ~12,742 km (7,918 miles)
  • Mass: ~5.97 × 10^24 kg
  • Surface Gravity: ~9.81 m/s²
  • Orbital Period: ~365.25 days (1 year)
  • Rotation Period: ~24 hours (1 day)

    • 🌐 Atmosphere:

    • Primary components:
    • Primary components:
    • Oxygen (~21%)
    • Nitrogen (~78%)

      • 🧭 Structure:

      • Core: Composed mainly of iron and nickel, with a solid inner core and a liquid outer core.
      • Mantle: A thick layer of semi-solid rock that flows slowly over geological timescales.
      • Crust: The thin outer layer of the Earth, where we live, consisting of continental and oceanic crust.

      🌎 Geology & Plate Tectonics:

      Earth’s lithosphere is divided into tectonic plates that float on the mantle. Their movement causes earthquakes, volcanic activity, and the formation of mountains.


      🌱 Life:

      Earth is the only planet confirmed to support life. The biosphere includes millions of species, with humans being the dominant life form.
